January 2013
David Smooke  started ArtMap Inc. to build cool internet things. In total, we started 100+ sites, but only one became the goodness greenness we know as Hacker Noon.
January 2016
Jay Zalowitz and David Smooke launched the Hacker Daily on Medium.com. Within two weeks, we published 1,000+ contributing writers
April 2016
Hacker Daily was rebranded and moved to HackerNoon.com: how hackers start their afternoons.
May 2017
Hacker Noon gained momentum, averaging 1,000,000 monthly readers. We expanded our editorial resources.
August 2017
Linh Dao Smooke joined the team. Weekly Sponsorship Package was launched, created a steady revenue without a paywalls, popups or personalized advertisements.
October 2017​
The essay, "Rick and Morty and the Meaning of Life" by Dan Jeffries was read by nearly every nerd on the internet because “Knowing the truth, that nothing matters, can actually save you in those moments."
November 2017
The data journalism story, “More than a Million Pro-Repeal Net Neutrality Comments were Likely Faked” by Jeff Kao was covered by every major tech outlet. Alyssa Milano even used it as evidence for why FCC Chairman Ajit Pai should not repeal Net Neutrality.
March 2018
Hacker Noon served as the SXSW Hackathon media partner as the company increased its in person media presence.
August 2018
The Weekly Sponsorship fully booked for a year, serving customers such as Pubnub, Manifold, Pusher, Segment, Mixpanel, DMarket, Odem, Full Stack Academy, and edX.
September 2018
Medium.com changed the terms on their content management system, forcing paywalls and blocking other site sponsorships. We rejected their offer to purchase HackerNoon.com.
November 2018
Hacker Noon kicked off its equity crowdfunding raise of $1,070,000  from 1,200+ readers (including Initialized's Alexis Ohanian & Garry Tan) at $6,400,000 pre-money valuation to build our own software.
December 2018
Google Cloud awarded us a $100,000 hosting grant to help us transition into our new infrastructure.
January 2019
Dane Lyons joined Hacker Noon as Chief Product Officer to lead the software team. We also expanded in editorial and business development.
July, 2019
Hacker Noon 2.0 launched, moving us from just a publishing to also a software company. Our own content management system powered the experience for readers, writers, editors, admins, and sponsors.
March 2020
Hacker Noon 2.1 launched with the NextJS Framework for a refreshed reader experience and vastly improved software architecture.
May 2020
Coil, the web monetization provider, led a $1,000,000 strategic investment in Hacker Noon at a $11,5000,000 pre-money valuation
